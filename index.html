FORUS POST-2030

✦ VISION ✦
The post-2030 conversation is not only about future goals. It is about participation, accountability, financing, and the political values that will shape international cooperation in the years ahead. That is why civil society must engage early, strategically, and collectively. Forus is launching a collective civil society vision on what must be defended, demanded, and declined as this debate unfolds. Find ways to engage in our social media calendar and toolkit here available in English, French, Spanish and Portuguese.
La conversación post-2030 no es solo sobre metas futuras. Se trata de participación, rendición de cuentas, financiamiento y los valores políticos que moldearán la cooperación internacional en los próximos años. Por eso la sociedad civil debe involucrarse temprano, estratégica y colectivamente. Forus está lanzando una visión colectiva de la sociedad civil sobre lo que debe defenderse, exigirse y rechazarse a medida que se desarrolla este debate. Launch: The Forus Post-2030 Vision
We kick off the campaign by presenting our 3Ds - what we defend, demand, and decline - as well as by sharing the event invitation to the launch of the Forus Post-2030 Vision report happening on May 21! The Forus post-2030 vision comes from our global civil society network of 74 National NGO Platforms and 8 Regional Coalitions, representing over 24,000 NGOs. It reflects collective reflection, lived realities, and voices often excluded from global agenda-setting. Join our multilingual social media campaign to explore these positions through posts, podcasts, and articles unpacking the vision.

🌍 Critical Questions for the Post-2030 Agenda
For this third week we focus on the report launch and a few critical questions guiding our vision. What is the one thing the next global development agenda must not lose? What are the biggest gaps the future framework must fix? How can we ensure, through stronger financing, real accountability, deeper localisation, and meaningful civic participation, that the next framework truly delivers? And ultimately, what makes a development agenda truly credible — rooted in ambition, firm accountability, and real implementation?
